What the pipeline does (step by step):

  • Step 1 — MediaPipe Face Detection: Finds the face bounding box and 6 landmarks (eyes, nose, ears, mouth) — replaces Haar Cascades
  • Step 2 — Face Crop + Preprocessing: Crops with configurable padding, applies CLAHE brightness normalization, bilateral smoothing
  • Step 3 — BiSeNet Face Segmentation: Neural network segments skin, brows, eyes, nose, lips into a precise mask (19-class model) — replaces circular crop
  • Step 4 — AnimeGANv2 Stylization: Transforms the real photo into an anime/cartoon style (Pixar-like effect)
  • Step 5 — Template Inpainting: Fills the white circle placeholder with surrounding pixel colors using Telea inpainting
  • Step 6 — Poisson Blending (seamlessClone): Blends the stylized face into the inpainted area with gradient-domain fusion — no harsh edges
  • Step 7 — Name Rendering: Renders the child's name with white outline for readability

Important note about the test image:

  • The test was run with a tiny 50×50 synthetic test face generated via code, not a real child photo
  • With a real photo, the AnimeGANv2 stylization + BiSeNet segmentation will produce significantly better results
